    1. Solution: Only compare yourself to your past self. Have your abilities and knowledge improved from where you were last week, last month, or last year? Be proud of the progress that you’ve made!

Asking for help

In essence, “help others help you!” This lesson will provide you with the tools to effectively ask questions in communities such as our Discord chat, Stack Overflow, and in the workplace!

Tips

  1. Always provide your code and the surrounding context.
    1. always include your code snippet
  1. Ask about the problem at hand, not the solution itself.
    1. Share your attempt.
      1. then you wont get your attempt as a suggestion.
    1. let people know that you are stuck
      1. Ask where to start researching
        1. you can also share pseudo-code to get a nudge in the right direction.
    1. people will ask for more context sometimes. respect that and dont take it to heart. They are volunteering information and generally want to help

When asking your question, please remember to include the context:

  • What do you think the problem is?
  • What exactly do you want to happen?
  • What is actually happening?
  • How did you get there?
  • What have you tried so far?

Helping others

  1. Instead of answering the question, guide them to the answer
  1. Help only when you are certain of the answer
  1. Help only when no one else is currently helping
  1. Help only when you have plenty of time
  1. Adjust your expectations to their level
    1. If the question does not reveal where they are in the curriculum, ask them so that you can adjust your expectations to their knowledge level.
  1. Ask for clarifications
  1. Ask for live code
    1. If the question needs to have live code to fully understand or debug, ask them use replit to provide it. If the problem is difficult to isolate, they should recreate the problem with isolated code.
  1. Do not answer questions covered in our curriculum.
  1. Answer the question before pointing out other problems
  1. Encourage students to use a debugger
  1. Watch for students that need to take a step back
  1. Watch for students that are in over their head
  1. Admit when the problem goes beyond your current knowledge
  1. Be patient
  1. Duck out of the conversation if you get frustrated
